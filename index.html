About
Add your photo here

I've spent my career at the intersection of data, product, and capital — usually before it was obvious that's where things were headed.

At Netflix, I spent five years building data as a product — not dashboards or reports, but infrastructure that engineering orgs could actually trust and act on. I worked on observability systems that gave teams real signal at scale, and contributed to $2M in cloud cost savings by treating data quality as a product problem, not an analytics one.

Before that and since, I've operated at the 0→1 stage — doing user research, scoping problems, shipping without a full team, and making product decisions with incomplete information. That tension between what founders need to know and what the data can actually tell them is something I understand from both sides.

I'm also an active angel investor in climate hardware and infrastructure through CladoVC, and an LP at Ganas Ventures. Sitting on the capital side has sharpened how I think about what makes a company fundable, what makes a product defensible, and where data and AI create durable advantage — versus where they're just overhead.

I advise founders who are building something technically complex and trying to figure out what to actually ship next.
